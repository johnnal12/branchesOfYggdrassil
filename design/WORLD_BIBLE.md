# Branches of Yggdrassil — World Bible

Reference for naming, lore, and tone. Use for item descriptions, environmental text, future content.

---

## Setting

Norse-mythology flavored fantasy. **Yggdrassil** — the World Tree — connects the nine realms. The game takes place in **Midgard**, the middle realm where humans and primal beasts coexist. Hunters are trained in the capital **Prontera** to venture into the wilds and deal with threatening creatures.

Tone: **warm fantasy with an undercurrent of danger**. Think *Monster Hunter* meets *Valheim* meets *RO nostalgia*. Not grimdark.

---

## Geography (MVP scope)

### Prontera — Capital of Rune-Midgarts
- Seat of King Tristan IV (successor to the legendary Tristan III)
- Walled stone city, cobblestone plazas, orange-tiled roofs
- Center: **Odin's Fountain** in Garibard Square
- Contains: Hunter's Guild (quest board), Royal Chivalry barracks, Temple of the Valkyrie
- *MVP: appears as title screen backdrop only*

### Field 08 — "The Verdant Hollows"
- Rolling plains southwest of Prontera
- Low-level hunting ground; novice hunters gain experience here
- Ancient ruins dotted throughout (pre-Midgard civilization remnants)
- Surrounded by Yggdrassil's southern roots, which occasionally break the ground
- *MVP: the fight arena*

## Bestiary

### Poring (small ambient, stretch-goal)
- **Species**: Gelatinous slime
- **Habitat**: Everywhere with moisture. Drawn to food scraps.
- **Threat level**: Minimal (novice test)
- **Appearance**: Translucent pink jelly, ~1 m diameter, vestigial arm-like protrusions, always appears to smile
- **Behavior**: Hops slowly toward anything warm. Bursts into pink mist when struck.
- **Lore note**: Porings are rumored to multiply by fission. Scholars debate whether they're a single organism or a hivemind. One of Rune-Midgarts' oldest and most endeared creatures.

### Peco Peco (hero boss)
- **Species**: Bird Wyvern (flightless)
- **Habitat**: Open plains, grasslands
- **Threat level**: Moderate (trained hunter should survive)
- **Appearance**: ~3 m tall, dark plumage with crimson accents, razor-sharp hooked beak, powerful clawed legs
- **Behavior**: Aggressive when threatened. Uses kicks, beak strikes, and feather-ruffling pressure waves. Can leap up to 8 m when enraged.
- **Lore note**: Once revered as pack steeds by early Midgard settlers, feral Peco clans have since returned to the wilds. Their feathers are prized by Hunter's Guild armorers for high-rank sets.

## Hunter's Guild

The **Hunter's Guild** is the Prontera-based organization that licenses and contracts hunters. New hunters (rank: **Novice**) are sent to Field 08 as their first trial.

The **Guild Master** is an unnamed figure in MVP. Post-MVP stretch goal: named NPC with voice lines.

### Hunter Classes (post-MVP)
- **Ranger** — bow-focused (player's class in MVP)
- **Swordsman** — melee-focused
- **Acolyte** — support/magic (future)
- **Merchant** — non-combat (future)

### Rank Progression (future)
Novice → Apprentice → Journeyman → Master → Grandmaster

## Item Name Seeds (for future)

When generating crafted items, pull from these conventions:

### Weapons
- Prefix: **Elder**, **Grand**, **Wyvern's**, **Runed**, **Ancient**, **Storm-**, **Frost-**
- Suffix: **Bite**, **Reach**, **Fang**, **Bane**, **Whisper**, **Song**

Examples:
- *Elder Wyvern's Reach* (bow)
- *Runed Poring Fang* (dagger — jokingly named)
- *Storm-Whisper Longbow*

### Armor
- Prefix: **Veteran's**, **Hunter's**, **Guild**, **Rune**, **Feathered**
- Suffix: **Vigil**, **Charge**, **Coat**, **Mantle**

### Items / Consumables
- **Yggdrassil Leaf** — full heal (classic RO reference)
- **Mjolnir Shard** — damage boost (mountain-reference)
- **Valkyrie Tear** — revive
- **Peco Plume** — crafting material

## Flavor Text Conventions

Short, evocative, Norse-warrior-mystic. **Not** flowery medieval.

Examples:

> *A feather plucked from a felled Peco. Still warm. The Guild's armorers will weave it into mail.*

> *It is said that when the Vanir spoke, Yggdrassil listened. These leaves bear the echo.*

> *Novice hunters die to Porings more often than anyone admits. Never underestimate a smile.*
